The Evolution of Web Technology

Static Languages and the Future of the Web

Posted on August 31, 2015 by Nick Pizzo

Much has been written on the topic of the future of the internet, and the changing nature of the typical interaction with web content. On many timelines, the surrounding and practices surrounding this regular part of life are still in their infancy, and one has to marvel at how quickly interacting with the internet has become a regular part of the human experience. Just like any exploding industry with a diverse and global market, many smart minds are working hard on furthering ease of web adoption.

An trend that may become more common is native browser support for techniques that now require external libraries. As mobile internet access continues to grow, responsive applications will be embraced and developers will have an easier experience implementing these techniques. HTML standards will catch up to the consensus of developer practices as this is perhaps the best feedback loop for the evolution of development.

Going forward, the user experience will be ever more removed from the inner-workings of the very tools used to access the internet. A larger number of home and office fixtures now have the ability to go online. This allows for collaboration among many points of connectivity, allowing user interaction to become more centralized and feature-rich.

Taking a look even further into the future is even more interesting. There is enough evidence to accurately say that hardware size will continue to decrease as micro computing becomes standardized. Interaction with software will resemble reality more and more, and personalied mobile virtual experiences will likely become common. As it has for several decades, web technology will offer a means of archiving human collaborative expression that enlightens and empowers the individual. The implications of this will continue to be realized, and they all offer a peek into vast worlds previously unimanginable. It's a great time to be a part of web technology.
